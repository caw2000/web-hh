Who We Are

Care Built on Integrity and Respect

🏠

Home is Where
Care Belongs

We believe adults with disabilities deserve skilled, dignified support in a true home environment — not an institution.

Honor Homes is a Minnesota-based residential care provider dedicated to serving adults with disabilities who require ongoing support in a home setting. We specialize in caring for individuals with complex medical and physical needs, including those who are primarily bed-bound.

Our team is trained to deliver consistent, high-quality care that respects each person's individuality and promotes their comfort, safety, and well-being. We work closely with case managers, families, and healthcare providers to ensure every resident receives the level of attention they deserve.

We are proud to be a trusted provider of state-funded waiver services, committed to meeting — and exceeding — all applicable licensing and care standards.

What We Offer

Our Care Services

Honor Homes provides two core service models, both designed to support adults with disabilities in a residential home environment.

FRS

Family Residential Services

FRS provides structured, long-term residential support for adults with disabilities living in a family-style home setting. Our staff provide hands-on assistance and supervision around the clock, ensuring continuity of care for individuals with significant physical or medical needs.

  • Assistance with activities of daily living (ADLs)
  • Medication management and health monitoring
  • Repositioning and personal hygiene care
  • Nutritional support and meal assistance
  • Coordination with medical and therapy providers
  • 24-hour supervision and emergency response
ICS

Integrated Community Supports

ICS is a flexible, home-based service model that supports adults with disabilities in living as independently as possible. Services are tailored to individual needs and are designed to promote community inclusion, skill-building, and a higher quality of everyday life.

  • Individualized support planning
  • Community participation and engagement
  • Independent living skill development
  • Transportation assistance
  • Health and wellness support
  • Coordination with waiver case managers
